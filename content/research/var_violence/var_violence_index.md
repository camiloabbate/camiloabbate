abstract: Using the staggered implementation of Video Assistant Referee (VAR) technology as a source of quasi-random variation, this paper analyzes the effects of increased scrutiny on players' violent behavior in soccer. By studying minute-by-minute match-level data from the top 5 European leagues (2008/2009–2019/2020), I examine how the distribution of fouls, yellow cards, and red cards changed at the player level following VAR adoption. Although focused on soccer, the findings have broader implications for understanding how heavily scrutinized environments influence violent behavior in strategic settings with high incentives at stake.
